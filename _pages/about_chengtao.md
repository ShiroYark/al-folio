Hi, I'm Chengtao — a postdoc in the [Ke Lab](https://medicine.yale.edu/profile/ailong-ke/) at Yale, where I spend most of my time figuring out how CRISPR proteins work and how to make them do new things.

My path here was a bit of a pivot. I did my PhD at Southeast University in Nanjing, where I worked on DNA-based data storage — essentially using DNA as a hard drive. It was a fun problem, but somewhere along the way I got hooked on structural biology and the idea of actually *seeing* how molecular machines operate. That curiosity led me to join Ailong Ke's lab as a postdoc, first at Cornell and then at Yale, where I've been using cryo-EM to study and engineer CRISPR-Cas systems. My recent work showed that you can strip down IscB and Cas9 into compact, reprogrammable nucleases that target single-stranded DNA and RNA — something that opens up a lot of interesting possibilities.

Outside the lab, I'm a big Formula 1 fan (yes, I do watch races at odd hours). I play badminton and basketball whenever I can drag people into it, and music is pretty much always on in the background — whether I'm pipetting or writing.
